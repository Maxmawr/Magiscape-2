    • Razoric replied on Feb 6, 2021

      KinematicBody if you want to control its position yourself (tween, animation player, position = ...)

      StaticBody is used for physics objects that do not move.

      RigidBody to have it be affected naturally by gravity, bounce, friction, etc without coding it yourself, likely the one you want.

      You'd basically spawn the coin (which would be a rigidbody2d), then when the coin is spawned, you'd also apply an impulse (apply_impulse()) of a certain strength so that it goes flying out. Then it'll naturally bounce off of walls and floors until it loses energy and stops moving, or gets picked up by the player.

      https://docs.godotengine.org/en/stable/classes/class_rigidbody2d.html

      This is the coin scene, where I change the Area2D node to a RigidBody2D. This allows basic gravity and physics. I had to add another CollisionShape2D to the RigidBody, which I made the same size as the coin sprite.

      I had to change the Layers and make sure the RigidBody2D masks the world, but not enemies etc.
